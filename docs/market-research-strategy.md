# PuckHub Market Research Strategy

## Finding Hockey Leagues to Contact as Users/Testers

### Primary Target: German-Speaking Amateur Ice Hockey Leagues

PuckHub's bilingual support (German primary, English secondary) makes German-speaking amateur leagues the warmest initial market.

#### Key Markets

- **Germany** — DEL2, Oberliga, Regionalliga, Landesliga, hobby/beer leagues
  - Deutscher Eishockey-Bund (DEB) — national federation with league directories
  - Landeseissportverbände — state federations (BEV Bavaria, LEV NRW, etc.)
- **Austria** — AEHL, regional amateur leagues
- **Switzerland** — lower-tier and amateur leagues

#### Where to Find Leagues

| Channel | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| Federation websites | League directories, operator contacts |
| Facebook groups | "Eishockey [Region]" — admins are often league organizers |
| Instagram/social | Small leagues posting manual score updates (pain signal) |
| Google search | `"Eishockey Liga" + [city/region]` — find league websites |
| Existing league websites | Outdated/ugly sites = prospects who need PuckHub |
| hockeyweb.de / eishockey.info | Community forums, league listings |
| eliteprospects.com | Lower-tier league directories with team/contact info |

### Identifying Best Prospects (Pain Signals)

Leagues most likely to adopt PuckHub are those experiencing problems the platform solves:

- **Manual stat tracking** (Excel, paper) — PuckHub automates stats
- **No website or outdated website** — PuckHub includes a website builder
- **Facebook-only online presence** — they need a proper web presence
- **Score reporting via WhatsApp/email** — PuckHub offers public game reports
- **No standings/schedule published online** — core PuckHub feature
- **Manual game recaps** — PuckHub generates AI-powered recaps

### Outreach Strategy

#### Phase 1: Free Beta Testers (5-10 leagues)

- Offer free access for one full season
- Target small leagues (4-8 teams) for manageable scope and fast feedback
- Prioritize tech-savvy, younger league operators who are active online
- Pitch: "We built this for leagues like yours — try it free, give us feedback"

#### Phase 2: Warm Referrals

- Ask beta testers to refer other league operators
- Hockey communities are tight-knit — word of mouth is the strongest channel

#### Phase 3: Federation Partnerships

- Approach state-level federations with case studies from beta testers
- Offer bulk/group pricing for all leagues under a federation umbrella

### Concrete First Steps

1. Search DEB and state federation websites for league directories and operator contacts
2. Join 5-10 Facebook groups for regional amateur hockey (Germany, Austria, Switzerland)
3. Identify 20 leagues with poor or nonexistent web presence
4. Draft a short pitch email in German highlighting: free website, automatic stats, AI game recaps
5. Set up a demo instance with realistic sample data for prospect demos
6. Post in hockey community forums (hockeyweb.de) offering free beta access

### Secondary Markets (Future)

- **Inline/roller hockey leagues** — similar league structure, underserved by software
- **English-speaking markets** — Canadian/US beer leagues (massive market, English locale already supported)
- **Adjacent sports** — floorball, handball, and other sports with similar league structures
